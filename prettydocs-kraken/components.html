Learning the Basics of Trading

If you already know the financial jargon of the crypto ecosystem, and just want to learn how to buy & sell, jump ahead to Placing Orders If you're a novice then you should read this section, as it will explain some useful financial terms.

Bid & Ask price

The Ask price is the lowest price that sellers are willing to sell at. When you want to buy cryptocurrency, you will buy at the current Ask price (Note: you may pay a higher price for some of your crypto, if there is not enough crypto available at the current Ask price. Let's say the current Ask price for Bitcoin is 30000 USD, and there are 11 Bitcoin available at the price. If you want to buy 12 Bitcoin, you will buy 11 Bitcoin at 30000 USD. The remaining part of your order (1 Bitcoin) will be bought at the next highest price).

Looking at the image of the Chart Menu, you can see the current Ask price is 29685.2 USD, which is at the bottom of the column of red numbers. The next seller is willing to sell at 29685.3 USD



The Bid price is the exact opposite of the Ask price. It's the highest price that buyers are willing to buy at. So if you're selling Bitcoin, the highest someone is going to buy it off you is at 29685.1 USD (see the green column of numbers in the image above)
