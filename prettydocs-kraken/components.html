Learning the Basics of Trading

If you already know the financial jargon of investing, and just want to learn how to buy & sell, jump ahead to Placing Orders If you're a novice then you should read this section, as it will explain some useful financial terms.

Ask & Bid price

The Ask price is the lowest price that sellers are willing to sell at. When you want to buy cryptocurrency, you will buy at the current Ask price (Note: you will pay a higher price for some of your crypto, if there isn't enough currency available at the current Ask price). Let's say the current Ask price for Bitcoin (BTC) is 29685.2 USD, and there is 1 BTC available at that price (Note: the amount available is called the Volume, so we have a Volume of 1 BTC at 29658.2 USD). If you want to buy 2 BTC, you will only be able to buy 1 BTC at 29685.2 USD. The remaining part of your order (1 BTC) will be bought at the next highest price).

Looking at the image of the Chart Menu, you can see the current Ask price is 29685.2 USD, which is at the bottom of the column of red numbers. The Volume of BTC is 1.39362018. If you want to buy 2 BTC, you will buy 1.39 BTC at 29685.2 USD and the remaining 0.61 BTC from the next seller at 29685.3 USD



The Bid price is the exact opposite of the Ask price. It's the highest price that buyers are willing to buy at. So if you're selling BTC, the highest price someone will buy it from you is at 29685.1 USD (see the green column of numbers in the image above)

Types of Orders

In order to buy or sell cryptocurrencies, you have to place an order. There are several types of order in the Kraken exchange:

Order List
  1. Market Order
  2. Limit Order
  3. Stop Loss
  4. Take Profit
  5. Stop Loss Limit Order
  6. Take Profit Limit Order

1. Market Order
A Market Order is the simplest type of order. It is an instant order where you specify the amount of currency you want to buy or sell. If you're buying, you will buy at the Ask price (in the manner we previously described), and if you're selling, you sell at the Bid price. When your order goes through and you make the purchase or sale, this is called "getting filled".

2. Limit Order
A Limit Order is useful when you want to buy or sell at a specific price. In the description of the Ask price, we saw that the current Ask price was 29685.2 USD with a Volume of 1.39362018. A seller in the market created a Limit Order stating that they wanted to sell 1.39362018 of BTC at 29685.2 USD, and this Limit Order was entered into the Order Book. Above that, in the Order Book, you can see that another seller created a Limit Order where they want to sell 5.05354733 BTC at 29685.3.

So you may have made a Market Order where you bought 1 BTC at 29685.2 USD. Now you want to sell it at 29695.2 USD to make a profit. You can create a Limit Order for this and the order will be put into the Order Book. It may take some time for the order to get filled depending on the movement of the market price, for instance, if there are more sellers than buyers in the market, the price of the crytocurrency could drop significantly, meaning that there is no demand to buy your BTC at such a high price.

3. Stop Loss
Again, there are two types of this order.

With a Sell Stop Loss Order, you can set a price to sell your crypto if the market moves against you. If you purchase 1 BTC at 29685 USD and set a Stop Loss of 29585 USD, then if the market for BTC crashes to 3000 USD, then in theory you should only lose 100 USD as the exchange will trigger a Sell Market Order when the current BTC price hits your Stop Loss.

For a Buy Stop Loss Order, it's essentially the opposite of the Sell Stop Loss Order. Let's say you sell 1 BTC at 29685 USD. You expect price to decrease dramatically and you estimate you'll be able to buy that 1 BTC back at a much lower price (say 28685 USD). However, the price of Bitcoin starts to sky rocket to 50000 USD. But you already set a Buy Stop Loss Order at 29785 USD, so the exchange triggers a buy order when this stop is reached, meaning you only lose 100 USD.
